Lemon Chicken Piccata

Chicken piccata is an Italian-American dish made with lightly floured and pan-fried chicken cutlets served in a lemon-butter sauce with capers. It's known for its bright, tangy flavor and rich, buttery finish. The dish is typically made by sautéing chicken breasts in olive oil or butter, then deglazing the pan with white wine, lemon juice, and chicken broth. Capers are added for a briny contrast, and the sauce is finished with more butter for a silky texture. It’s often served with pasta, rice, or vegetables.
